Renewable Technology Challenge

For this project, my team worked to design a custom wind turbine that can harness kinetic energy from the rooftops of civilians' homes. The goal is to develop a wind turbine that produces energy that ensures maximum efficiency and reliability while prioritizing durability, portability, and affordability. This must all be done while ensuring that the size and weight of the design are small enough to fit on a residential rooftop easily and the blade material can withstand Calgary's high wind conditions and environmental factors.

Health Care Challenge

In this project, we focused on remote sensing and actuation for robotic arms in surgical applications. Our team worked to design a system to transfer a surgical instrument to an autoclave for sterilization securely. We applied the principles of rapid prototyping and physical computing to develop a computer program and prototype a container with certain specifications. We researched the core requirements for a surgical sterilization system and used those concepts to create our solution. As a member of the coding team I worked to create a program that will sort the containers (that appear at random) and place them in the correct location. This is virtual environment is similar to how sterilization sorting could be executed in the real world.

Sustainability Challenge

For this project, my team developed a robotic sorting system to classify containers by material, mass, and contamination utilizing Qbots, sensors, and Python. Our goal was to design a system for sorting and recycling containers to filter different materials and contamination levels effectively. This allows recycling plants to execute the proper recycling processes and limit the amount of waste harming our planet

Community Challenge

This project was a part of the IMPACT (Interdisciplinary, Mentorship, Practice, Applied, Community, Transformative) initiative, and through this process, we were introduced to our amazing clients, Tim and Kim. Specifically, my team focused on Tim and products that would help support his visual impairment. So, throughout the past few months, both in and outside design studios, my team and I worked together to design and create a product to help him better use his television remote. Currently, he often relies on muscle memory when using his TV remote and cannot utilize many functions. So, by incorporating an audibility function through touch sensors, we created a device that will allow him to hear each button's functionality before pushing it. Our overall device was a durable, audible, and easy-to-use product that improved the accessibility of how he used the television remote in his home.
